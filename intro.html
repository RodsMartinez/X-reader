It's time to wake up






An intense, blinding migraine.
Foam gushed from my mouth.
It felt like millions — no, billions — of red ants squirming inside my brain.
My head was wracked with pain.
If I had a choice between this and death...
I would have begged for death.
""
Suddenly, the pain eased.
A voice — distant, mechanical — called out to me.
"It seems you're awake, ."
A figure leaned into view.
A woman.
Or at least, something shaped like one.
Her appearance was... haunting.
Not ugly — no, far from it.
She was perfect.
Too perfect.
Her face was smooth, flawless, unnervingly symmetrical.
Her voice buzzed with a faint metallic hum.
(? Was that supposed to be my name?)
"How long... was I out?"
"Ten years. Exactly."
Ten years...?
The words barely registered.
My throat burned as I croaked out the next question.
"Who... are you?"
"It seems the extraction has taken a toll on your physical body.
Fortunately, your mind remains intact."
"What... are you talking about?"
"I will be your guide, ."
"My name is Helena Theresa M. Lewis. You may call me HTML."
"I am an android, assigned to guide you through the Protocol."
An android?
A protocol?
What the hell was she talking about?
"You will now begin fulfilling your service."
A service?
What service?
Before I could even protest, she continued —
dropping the real deal.
"We need you to save this nation."
"Huh...? What...?"
Without warning, she reached out —
her cold, metal fingers brushing across my forehead.
"And your mind is the key."
She extended her hand toward me —
waiting for me to take it.
"Because you, , are the well-proclaimed X Reader."

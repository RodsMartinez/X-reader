I didn’t have time to think.

No strategy, no plan.

Just raw rage—ugly, blistering rage—boiling over everything else.

The guilt, the loss, the helplessness—it all collapsed into one violent instinct.

With a roar, I charged Ricci.

My fists clenched so tight I could feel the bones grind.

My heart slammed against my ribs like it wanted out.

Ricci didn’t flinch.

He didn’t even move.

Just watched me with that same smug, hollow stare—like a man who’s already won.

Then—crack.

I hit the pavement.

Face first.

Hard.

Breath gone.

Vision swimming.

Ricci crouched beside me, his voice low, almost casual.

“You never were good at thinking things through, Doc.”

I tried to move.

Tried to speak.

Nothing.

He leaned in close.

I could smell the cologne, the smoke on his jacket.

“Another goddamn footnote in my story.”

He stood up.

Lit a cigarette with steady hands.

The flick of the lighter echoed like a gunshot.

Then he walked away. Just like that.

The street was quiet again.

No sirens.

No help coming.

Just the sound of my own blood soaking into the concrete.

And for the first time, I understood:

This wasn’t justice.

This was just how it ends.

You failed.

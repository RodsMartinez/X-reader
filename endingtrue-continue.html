I looked between Ricci and the X-Reader.

HTML’s voice echoed calmly, like it always did in the darkest moments.

“His body is reliant on energy.”

“To terminate his movements…”

“you must disable the X-Reader itself.”

I hesitated.

“Will it work?”

“Yes.”

Not a single trace of doubt.

That was enough.

I turned and ran—not toward Ricci,

but to the core.

He shouted behind me,

his voice twisted with desperation.

“Wait—”

“Wait—wait!”

“You don’t know what you’re doing!”

“If you disable it then—”

I didn’t listen. I couldn’t.

All I saw was the console,

the pulsing heart of this cursed place.

The override sequence blinked red,

waiting for me to decide.

I hit the keys.

The system began to hum violently.

Alarms screamed.

Sparks burst from his body,

the energy sustaining him unraveling.

He was nothing without it.

A parasite detached from its host.

He let out one last scream—.

and shut down the moment the power went down.

I turned to Hadley,

a breath of relief escaping me.

“It’s over.”

I said with the brightest smile I could ever muster

But she didn’t move.

“Hadley…?”

She swayed slightly.

Her limbs twitched,

like wires were misfiring.

My stomach turned.

“What’s wrong?”

Her voice crackled as her systems began to fail.

“Disabling the X-Reader…”

“removes my power sources”

I froze...

“No… wait, I didn’t know about tha—”

“I’m… sorry. ”

“I didn’t want to risk hesitation.”

She looked up at me,

eyes soft and flickering like a dying star.

“It had to be done.”

I dropped to my knees beside her.

“No...”

“No, no, no, no,”

we can fix this—”

"we can probably reroute something—I didn’t mean for this—!”

She raised a hand,

brushed my face with fingers growing colder.

“You did the right thing.”

A pause.

“You always did.”

“In every life.”

She smiled—softly, peacefully.

The first time it looked truly human.

Then her hand fell.

Her eyes dimmed.

And she was gone.

The room didn’t notice.

No alarms. No shutdown tone.

Just the hum of old circuits, still running.

I waited for her voice to return.

For a flicker. A blink. Anything.

But there was only stillness.

I touched her hand. It didn’t respond.

Not frozen. Not lifeless. Just... still.

I had never noticed how quiet the world could be without her.

She wasn’t a machine. Not to me. Not anymore.

I stayed there, knees to the floor, hand in hers—

—like if I held on long enough, I could stop time from finishing what it started.

—⧗—

The world was free now.

Ricci was gone.

The X-Reader...

...dismantled.

...the war seemed to be over

But I couldn’t bring her back.

I never even got to say goodbye.

All I could do was remember.

Her voice.
Her presence.
The way she always stood beside me.
Always...
...In every life.
